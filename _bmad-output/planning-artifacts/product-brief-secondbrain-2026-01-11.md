# Product Brief: secondbrain

## Executive Summary

**secondbrain** is a intelligent note-taking and knowledge management system designed for individuals drowning in fragmented information across multiple platforms (paper, iPad, markdown files, mobile devices). Rather than imposing rigid organization structures, secondbrain enables a natural workflow: **Capture → refinement (Refinement) → Exploitation (Insights)**. It becomes a true second brain that learns your context, connects disparate notes, and generates powerful, actionable weekly insights through an Eisenhower matrix lens.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

For 10 years, Matthieu has struggled with fragmented note-taking across multiple mediums: paper notebooks (for cognitive rest), iPad (with Apple Pen), markdown files (for their elegance and exportability), GDocs, and sometimes just memory. This fragmentation creates critical friction:

- **Capture is easy, but retrieval is nightmare.** Notes scatter everywhere, context gets lost.
- **Manual synthesis is broken.** Weekly summaries to communicate accomplishments to leadership are "bancales" (shaky) and incomplete.
- **Knowledge doesn't compound.** Important learnings and bookmarks from the internet disappear into the void—no efficient recall or connection-finding.
- **Current tools fail.** Notion, Obsidian, Roam excel at capture but fail at organization without creating infinite tag lists or decision-making paralysis about hierarchies that shift over time.

The core frustration: **Tools force you to optimize for their structure, not for your thinking.**

### Problem Impact

This fragmentation has cascading consequences across three critical life areas:

1. **Professional Growth Blocked**
   - Weak synthesis means weak communication to leadership about accomplishments
   - Missed promotion opportunities due to inability to articulate impact clearly
   - Knowledge doesn't accumulate—re-asking for information, losing details, approximating work quality

2. **Learning Velocity Stagnated**
   - Bookmarks and online discoveries never surface again
   - Lack of connection-finding prevents deeper learning and insight generation
   - Weekly context switching between different note systems burns mental cycles

3. **Personal Projects Languish**
   - Business, vacation, hobby projects lack the supporting knowledge infrastructure
   - Ideas and progress are scattered, making momentum impossible to sustain

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Current tools (Notion, Obsidian, Roam, Apple Notes, etc.) solve the capture problem brilliantly but fail at exploitation:

- **Organization complexity.** Tags multiply infinitely. Hierarchies shift over time. Users spend energy organizing instead of thinking.
- **Search imprecision.** Keyword search without context isn't intelligent enough for real-world queries ("show me everything about client X" or "what have I learned about Y?").
- **Digest failure.** Generated task lists are overwhelming noise—hundreds of "ideas" that aren't real actions. No distinction between fleeting thoughts and actionable next steps.
- **Inflexibility.** These tools don't adapt to how your criteria and context evolve. You're stuck with whatever structure you built weeks ago.
- **Integration friction.** iPad notes, markdown files, mobile captures, and browser clips don't flow together seamlessly.

### Proposed Solution

**secondbrain** reimagines the workflow with three integrated phases:

**1. CAPTURE (Frictionless Input)**
- Write in your preferred medium: iPad handwriting (with Apple Pen), markdown (desktop/mobile), or voice
- The system receives raw input + basic metadata (date, source)
- Zero upfront organization required. Zero cognitive load.

**2. refinement (Weekly Refinement - 30 minutes)**
- Review your week's notes once, at a dedicated time (Friday afternoon)
- The system suggests:
  - **Connections:** "This note links to client X (you mentioned them on Jan 5)"
  - **Note types:** Is this an idea, accomplishment, learning, meeting note, or actionable task?
  - **Context/tags:** Inferred from content and history (user validates)
  - **Related insights:** Patterns that emerge from your notes
- You accept, reject, or refine suggestions—taking 30 minutes to transform raw captures into connected knowledge
- The system now understands the full picture

**3. EXPLOITATION (Powerful Insights)**
- **Weekly digest** generated automatically:
  - Accomplishments of the week
  - Hard points to address next week
  - Critical reminders (Eisenhower matrix perspective)
- **Intelligent search:** Fuzzy search by client, project, date, or natural language questions ("What did I learn about feature X?")
- **AI-powered Q&A:** Ask questions in natural language; the system retrieves and synthesizes answers from your entire knowledge base
- **Knowledge compounding:** The more notes you capture and refine, the better the system understands you

### Key Differentiators

1. **Organization without friction.** No forced hierarchies or tag management. The system infers structure from your patterns.

2. **The refinement layer.** Rather than fully automated (too rigid) or fully manual (too laborious), the 30-minute weekly refinement with smart suggestions hits the sweet spot—you stay in control while the system does heavy lifting.

3. **Distinction between ideas and actions.** While other tools generate endless task lists, secondbrain filters noise and surfaces only real next steps—distinguishing fleeting ideas from concrete actions.

4. **Flexible capture, unified exploitation.** Write however you want (iPad, markdown, mobile). The backend unifies everything and makes it searchable and connectable.

5. **Evolves with you.** As your context and criteria shift, the system learns and adapts—it doesn't require you to rebuild everything.

## Success Metrics

### User Success Metrics

**Primary Success Indicator:**
User has formed a sustainable note-taking habit and is receiving genuine value from the system.

**Metric 1: Daily Capture Habit**
- **Target:** Minimum 1 note per weekday (Monday-Friday) = 10 notes/week
- **Why it matters:** Indicates user is actually using the system as intended, not abandoning it after initial excitement
- **Measurement:** Automatic tracking of note count per week
- **Success threshold:** Sustained for 4+ weeks

**Metric 2: Weekly Refinement Completion**
- **Target:** Completing refinement step within 30 minutes every Friday
- **Why it matters:** Shows user is engaging with the intelligent suggestions and converting raw captures into structured knowledge
- **Measurement:** Time spent in refinement session + number of suggestions accepted/rejected
- **Success threshold:** 4/5 weeks completed per month

**Metric 3: Actionable Digest Generation**
- **Target:** Generating digestible, actionable weekly summary by Monday morning
- **Why it matters:** Digest must be polished enough to share with leadership or use for personal planning
- **Measurement:** Digest generated + user review/approval time
- **Success threshold:** Consistent weekly, high quality (not just a data dump)

**Metric 4: Search Effectiveness**
- **Target:** Searches return relevant results (quality over quantity)
- **Why it matters:** Core feature—if search doesn't work, the system fails its promise
- **Measurement:** User satisfaction with search results (implicit: revisiting searches, refining queries)
- **Success threshold:** User successfully answers 8/10 search queries first try

**Metric 5: Knowledge Retention & Compounding**
- **Target:** User discovering connections and patterns they didn't remember capturing
- **Why it matters:** Indicates system is actually becoming a "second brain"—improving over time, not just storing data
- **Measurement:** User commenting on unexpected connections found during search
- **Success threshold:** 3+ "aha" moments per month

### Strategic Note on Metrics

The goal of secondbrain is **not** to optimize for vanity metrics (e.g., most notes captured, most searches performed). Instead, metrics should demonstrate:

1. **Habit formation** - You're using it regularly enough it becomes automatic
2. **Value creation** - The system is delivering on its promise (digests, search, insights)
3. **Knowledge compounding** - The system improves over time as it learns you
4. **Sustainability** - You're willing to maintain the 30-min weekly refinement because it pays off

If these metrics are met after 3 months, the product is working. Then, exploration of SaaS potential becomes viable.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features (Phase 1)

#### **1. Note Capture Interface**

**What's Included:**
- Web-based note creation interface (simple, distraction-free)
- Markdown as the primary format and source of truth
- Direct input: users type/paste markdown directly in the application
- Basic rich-text preview alongside editor (optional markdown preview)
- Automatic timestamping of captures
- Simple note list/history view

**Implementation Details:**
- One page, minimal design
- Create new note → Edit in markdown → Save
- Notes stored in application database

**Out of Scope for MVP:**
- File uploads (will convert external formats to markdown in Phase 2)
- iPad stylus input / handwriting capture (Phase 2)
- Voice-to-text capture (Phase 2)
- Web clipper / browser extension (Phase 2)

---

#### **2. Intelligent refinement (Refinement) System**

**What's Included:**
- Weekly refinement interface (Friday focus)
- System analyzes captured notes and generates suggestions:
  - **Connection Suggestions:** "This note links to [previous note/context]"
  - **Note Type Classification:** Idea, Accomplishment, Learning, Meeting Notes, Actionable Task
  - **Tag/Context Inference:** Suggested tags based on content and history
  - **Pattern Recognition:** Observations of emerging themes in the week's captures
- User validation workflow:
  - Review each suggestion: Accept, Reject, or Modify
  - Manual editing of tags/types
  - Add notes or context to suggestions
- Metadata update: Once refined, notes are enriched with validated metadata

**Implementation Details:**
- Suggestions powered by:
  - Simple keyword/phrase matching (Phase 1)
  - Semantic similarity (Phase 1)
  - User feedback history (improving over time)
- Notes become "structured" after refinement (metadata captured)
- Clear visual feedback on which notes have been refined vs. raw

**Why This is Critical:**
- refinement transforms raw captures into interconnected knowledge
- Suggestions reduce manual work while keeping user in control
- This layer is essential for Phase 2 (Exploitation) to be powerful

**Out of Scope for MVP:**
- Fully automated AI refinement (human-in-the-loop is the feature)
- Advanced NLP/ML models (simple heuristics first)
- Real-time suggestions during capture (weekly batch process only)

---

#### **3. Simple Note Review & Search (Pre-Exploitation)**

**What's Included:**
- View all notes with refinement status
- Simple search by keyword
- Filter by note type (after refinement)
- Basic sorting (date, type)
- Read-only preview of notes
- Markdown rendering for comfortable reading

**Why:**
- Allows you to read/review notes end-of-week
- Validates that refinement is working
- Foundation for Phase 2 search and digests

**Out of Scope for MVP:**
- Advanced search (fuzzy, semantic)
- Digests / weekly summaries
- Knowledge graphs or connection visualizations
- Cross-note insights

### MVP Success Criteria

**MVP is successful when:**

1. **Capture works reliably**
   - You can create, edit, and save markdown notes easily
   - Notes persist correctly in the system
   - No friction or friction is minimal

2. **refinement delivers value**
   - Suggestions are mostly relevant (8/10 hit rate)
   - You're refining 80%+ of weekly notes
   - Refinement takes <30 minutes as targeted
   - You see patterns emerging from suggestions

3. **Knowledge is findable**
   - You can search notes and find what you're looking for
   - Reading notes end-of-week is easy and pleasant
   - Metadata (type, tags) from refinement helps organization

4. **Habit formation begins**
   - You're capturing at least 10 notes/week consistently
   - Friday refinement becomes a ritual
   - You notice you're not losing context anymore

5. **Confidence for Phase 2**
   - Clear signal that Exploitation layer will be valuable
   - Abundant refined data ready for digests/search/insights
   - You're excited to build the next phase

**Go/No-Go Decision Point:** After 4 weeks of MVP usage
- If all above criteria met: Proceed to Phase 2 (Exploitation)
- If engagement low or suggestions poor: Iterate on MVP before expanding
- If capture/refinement flow is broken: Fix before Phase 2
